Discover the Wonders of the Brain

The brain is the most complex organ in the human body, responsible for all functions of the body and mind.

Introduction

The brain is the central organ of the human nervous system, housed within the skull. It is responsible for processing sensory information, regulating bodily functions, and enabling cognition and behavior.

Structure of the Brain

The brain is divided into several regions, each responsible for different functions. These include the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem.

Functions of the Brain

The brain controls thought, memory, emotion, touch, motor skills, vision, breathing, temperature, and many other crucial functions.

Interesting Facts

The human brain has approximately 86 billion neurons. It consumes about 20% of the body's energy despite only representing about 2% of its weight.

Detailed Breakdown

  • Cerebrum

    The cerebrum is the largest part of the brain and is divided into left and right hemispheres. It is responsible for higher brain functions such as thought and action.

  • Cerebellum

    The cerebellum is located under the cerebrum and is involved in the coordination of voluntary movements, balance, and posture.

  • Brainstem

    The brainstem connects the brain to the spinal cord and controls many automatic functions of the body, including breathing and heart rate.
